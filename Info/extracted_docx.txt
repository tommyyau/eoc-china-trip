A Hiking Tour to Xi’An, Beijing, Jiangxi, and Shandong China
(An exciting journey that blends stanning natural scenery, cultural heritage and outdoor challenges.)

2. Detailed Itinerary

17th May 22:00 flight from Heathrow, arrive Xi’An on 18th May by Tianjin Airline, met by our professional English speaking guide, transfer to the city, visit to Big Wild Goose Pagoda if time permits, check in at Easter House Xi’an. ( Breakfast, Dinner)

19th May Departure for City Walk in the old town center after hotel breakfast, including the Bell Tower and Drum Tower. Visit the Muslim Quarter and Great Mosque. Spend the afternoon hiking on the 600 year-old City Wall which runs 13.7km around the old city center， The city wall Xi’an is one of the largest and best-preserved ancient city walls in existence in China, and it also serves as a landmark historical relic of Xi'an. Enjoy Dumpling Banquet dinner.

20th May Morning tour at the Terracotta Warrior Museum, lunch at local Chinese restaurant, afternoon hiking at Daming Palace Heritage Park.

The Terracotta Army of Emperor Qin Shi Huang The Terracotta Army is one of the most significant archaeological discoveries of the 20th century. Located in Xi'an, Shaanxi Province, China, it is a collection of thousands of life-sized terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. China Compass Travel Limited Add. Room 3001, Dongfang Hotel, #120, Liu Hua Road510016 Guangzhou China Daming Palace National Heritage Park The Daming Palace National Heritage Park in Xi'an is the site of the former Daming Palace (the "Palace of Great Brilliance"), the grandest imperial palace complex of the Tang Dynasty. During its heyday (7th-8th century), it was the political center of the empire and the residence of Tang emperors, symbolizing the peak of ancient Chinese civilization.

21st May Morning tour to the Beilin Museum. Lunch at local Chinese restaurant. Transfer to the train station / airport for departure to Beijing.

Arrive at a Beijing railway station; private transfer to a centrally located 4-star hotel for convenience for subsequent activities. Check in and settle into hotel.
Evening: Welcome dinner at 5:00 PM, featuring meetings and exchanges with representatives from China’s hiking and mountaineering communities. A brief introduction to Chinese mountaineering culture and safety guidelines will be provided.

22nd May: Cultural Exploration and Warm up Hike in Beijing

· Morning: Visit Forbidden City (故宫博物院). Explore along the central axis of Beijing, including the Hall of Supreme Harmony （太和殿）, Hall of Central Harmony （中和殿）, and Hall of Preserving Harmony （保和殿））, immersing in the history and imperial architecture of the Ming and Qing dynasties. It is recommended to enter early to avoid crowds, and expert guided commentary will be arranged for deeper understanding.
· Afternoon: Hiking from the National Botanical Garden （国家植物园） to Happy Forest in Fragrant Hills （香山） (12 km, 670 m elevation). This warmup hike prepares the body for upcoming challenges. Chinese mountaineers and members of Beijing outdoor sports clubs will accompany the group, provide safety support and introduce local flora and geography.
· Evening: Return to the downtown hotel; dinner can feature Beijing specialties such as Peking duck.

23rd May: Great Wall Hiking Challenge

· Full Day: Hike along the Badaling Great Wall (8–10 km, ~500 m elevation). Experience the pride of “He who has never been to the Great Wall is not a true man.” In 1986, Queen Elizabeth II climbed the Badaling Great Wall on her 60th birthday and remarked, “I’ve been to many places, but the Great Wall of China is the most magnificent”.  Guides will explain the history and architectural ingenuity of the Great Wall. Remember to apply sun protection cream, bring ample water and energy snacks.
· Evening: Overnight sleeper train to Lushan—comfortable, efficient, and time-saving, ensuring adequate rest.

24th May: Arrival in Jiangxi, First Glimpse of Lushan
· Morning: Private transfer from Jiujiang Station; fresh up and have breakfast at the hotel 
· Morning: Take a cable car to the mountain-top hotel on Lushan for check-in. 
  Note: The mountain-top architecture of Lushan was primarily developed by the British missionary Edward Selby Little. In 1895, he discovered Lushan’s pleasant climate and built villas, attracting foreign merchants and missionaries to purchase land and construct summer residences, developing into the early Lushan villa community.
· Afternoon: Leisurely 8 km hike along Lushan’s trails, warm up to the altitude and appreciating cultural landmarks such as Meilu Villa and historical academies.
· Evening: Dinner at the mountain-top hotel, featuring Lushan’s “Three Stones” and other local specialties.

25th May: Conquering Lushan’s Hero Slope

· Full Day: Summit hike on Lushan (~8 km, 900 m total elevation). Challenge the classic Hero Slope route and enjoy breathtaking views from the summit. Recommendations: Assess participants’ physical condition, ensure guided leadership, and practice step-walking techniques.
· Evening: Rest at the mountain-top hotel and share hiking experiences.

26th May: Natural Wonders and the Porcelain Capital

· Morning: Transfer to Hukou County for a hike on Mount Shizhong (10 km). Admire the spectacular confluence of the Yangtze River and Poyang Lake, experiencing the natural wonder of “Water striking stone, resonating like a bell.”
· Afternoon: Visit the “Porcelain Capital” of Jingdezhen and try hands-on ceramic activities such as wheel throwing and painting.
· Evening: Check into a hotel in Jingdezhen or Wuyuan.

27th May: Hiking Through Wuyuan’s Rural Landscapes

· Full Day: Hike in Wuyuan (7–12 km). Explore Jujing Village, Wolong Valley (waterfall clusters), Zhang Village (bench bridge), and Moon Bay (sunset). Experience the pastoral beauty and poetic landscapes of China’s most beautiful countryside. Recommendations: Travel light, carry rain gear, and ensure cameras are fully charged.
· Evening: Stay at a characteristic homestay or hotel in Wuyuan.

28th May: Huangling Terraces and Immortal Valley Night Views

· Morning: Visit Huangling in Wuyuan to see typical Hui-style mountain villages and fan-shaped terraces.
· Afternoon to Evening: Transfer to Wangxian Valley in Shangrao. Hike approximately 10 km (300 m total elevation). Follow recommended routes, with a highlight on the dreamy, fairy-tale night views with the surrounding cliffs illuminated. Recommendations: Stay within or near Wangxian Valley to fully experience the night and morning scenery.
· Evening: Check into a characteristic homestay in Wangxian Valley or a hotel in Shangrao.


29th May: High-Speed Rail to Shandong, Preview of Tai’An

· Morning: Take high-speed rail from Shangrao to Tai’An, Shandong (~4–5 hours). Private transfer from Tai’An Station.
· Afternoon: Visit Dai Temple after lunch (including Tiankuang Hall, Han Cypress Court, East Imperial Lodging, and Stele Forest). This was where ancient emperors worshipped the God of Mount Tai and held Fengshan ceremonies, serving as the spiritual core of Mount Tai worship. A professional guide will provide in-depth historical and cultural context, setting the stage for the next day’s climb.
· Evening: Check into a hotel in Tai’An; prepare equipment and rest early.

30th May: Ascending Mount Tai, the Foremost of the Five Great Mountains

· Full Day: Climb Mount Tai. Choose a route based on physical condition:
  · Classic Challenge Route (recommended): Hongmen Entrance to Zhongtian Gate to Nantian Gate and Jade Emperor Peak (~7.5 km, ~1,300 m elevation, 4–6 hours).
  · Leisurely Experience Route: Transport from Tianwai Village to Zhongtian Gate, then hike or take a cable car to Nantian Gate.
    Recommendations: Start early to avoid heat and crowds; use trekking poles and knee supports for the “Eighteen Bends”; bring windproof clothing for temperature changes at the summit. The hotel will prepare trail lunches, fruits, and beverages for everyone.
· Evening: Enjoy a hearty dinner and stay at a hotel near Mount Tai.

31st May: Pilgrimage to Confucian Cultural Sites in Qufu

· Morning: After descending, transfer to the “Eastern Holy Cit” of Qufu (~1.5 hours).
· Full Day: In-depth visit to the “Three Confucian Sites” UNESCO World Heritage sites:
  · Confucius Temple: The primary temple for worshipping Confucius, listed among China’s three greatest ancient architectural complexes alongside the Beijing Forbidden City and Chengde Mountain Resort.
  · Confucius Mansion: The residence of Confucius’s direct descendants, the “Dukes of Yansheng” combining official offices and living quarters.
  · Confucius Forest: The dedicated cemetery of Confucius and his family, the world’s longest-continuously-used family cemetery.
    A knowledgeable cultural guide will provide deep insights. Participate in cultural activities such as a “Teacher Worship Ceremony.”
· Evening: Savor local specialties and stay at a hotel in Qufu.

1st June: Travel to Qingdao, Departure from Qingdao 15:00 to Heathrow.

· Morning: Transfer to Qingdao (transportation to be confirmed)
· Arrive Qingdao International Airport around 12:00. Departing from Qingdao to Heathrow at 15:00 (Tianjin Airline)
· Reminder: Arrive at the airport at least 3 hours before international flights for check-in procedures.
